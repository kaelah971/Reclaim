# Reclaim Brand Messaging

## Executive decision

**Reclaim** is the chosen brand name.

It is not ideal as a purely proactive payment name—it naturally evokes getting money back after something has failed—but it can work if the brand consistently reframes reclaiming as **retaining control over a payment when trust breaks down**.

Do not use **Reclaim Express**. “Express” makes a protection product sound hurried and transactional. The master brand is simply:

> **Reclaim**

## Brand core

### One-line description

> **Reclaim protects cUSD payments between clients and independent workers with clear terms, delivery evidence, fair review, and on-chain settlement.**

### Brand statement

> Reclaim turns a payment into a shared agreement. Before money moves, both sides define the work, deadline, release rule, and evidence expected. Funds stay protected until the work is approved—or, if something goes wrong, the case is prepared clearly for review and settled on Celo.

### Core promise

> **Move money without giving up your recourse.**

### Recommended tagline

> **Pay with proof.**

This is the strongest public line for Reclaim. It is short, practical, relevant to both buyer and seller, and keeps the product from sounding like a legal recovery service.

### Supporting lines

- **Clear terms. Protected funds.**
- **Work delivered. Money released.**
- **When trust is not enough, use terms and proof.**
- **AI prepares the case. People decide.**
- **A fair record for every payment.**

## Positioning

> For clients and independent digital workers who need to pay across borders without blind trust, Reclaim is a protected cUSD payment layer that holds funds to clear terms and delivery evidence. Unlike an irreversible wallet transfer or an informal agreement in chat, Reclaim creates one shared Payment Room, uses AI to prepare neutral dispute packets, and settles the agreed outcome on Celo.

## Messaging architecture

### Roof: the message to remember

> **Pay with proof.**

### Pillar 1: Terms before funds

**Message:** Reclaim makes both sides agree on what the payment is for before money is deposited.

**Proof:** explicit deliverable, amount, deadline, release rule, dispute window, evidence expectation, seller acceptance and an immutable payment record after funding.

### Pillar 2: Protection built into the payment

**Message:** Funds are protected by the agreed workflow—not by hoping the other party behaves well.

**Proof:** cUSD escrow, defined lifecycle, frozen funds during dispute, clear cancellation/expiry handling and on-chain settlement.

### Pillar 3: A fair record when things go wrong

**Message:** Reclaim turns a messy disagreement into a legible case, without pretending AI is the judge.

**Proof:** shared timeline, evidence map, AI-neutral case packet, human reviewer vote, contract execution and human-readable receipt.

### Pillar 4: Useful agentic payments, not AI theatre

**Message:** Every paid AI action produces something a person can use to protect a real payment.

**Proof:** x402 Terms Risk Check, Evidence Strength Check and Dispute Packet; each names its price, output and evidence basis before purchase.

## Injective/Celo-aligned hackathon narrative

Reclaim is being built for Celo’s Agentic Payments & DeFAI Hackathon. The sponsor story is not “we added AI and blockchain.” It is:

> **Reclaim makes stablecoin commerce usable when trust is incomplete: cUSD moves into escrow, agents prepare useful protection actions through x402, people review disputes, and Celo settles a visible outcome.**

| Celo / hackathon requirement | Reclaim expression |
|---|---|
| Real on-chain money movement | cUSD deposit, escrow, settlement, fee and optional reviewer reward routing |
| x402 payments | Paid terms-risk report and dispute-packet generation |
| Agentic utility | AI structures terms, evidence and neutral case packets; it does not decide the winner |
| Global/mobile relevance | Low-fee, mobile-first protection for remote workers and clients |
| Real product potential | A plug-in protection layer for Celo checkout, payment links and marketplaces |

## Voice

### Voice principles

| Principle | Meaning | Sounds like | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calmly exact | Explain money, rules and state without drama | “Funds are protected until the delivery deadline or your approval.” | “Your funds are moon-safe.” |
| Fair to both sides | Never frame buyer or seller as automatically suspicious | “Seller evidence is ready for review.” | “Catch unreliable sellers.” |
| Human before technical | Translate on-chain activity into practical consequences | “100 cUSD is now held under these terms.” | “Escrow transaction finalised at block…” as the first message |
| Honest about AI | State what the agent did and did not decide | “AI organised the case; reviewers decide the outcome.” | “AI found the truth.” |
| Firm in critical states | Be unambiguous about deadlines, restrictions and finality | “This payment is frozen while the dispute is reviewed.” | “We are looking into it.” |

### Vocabulary

Use: **protected payment, Payment Room, terms, funds protected, delivery, evidence, review, settlement, receipt, cUSD, clear, shared record.**

Avoid: **refund miracle, win your case, crypto revolution, trustless magic, frictionless, degen, guaranteed, automated justice, AI judge, chargeback killer.**

## Message tests

Test these before submitting:

1. Can a client explain Reclaim without using the word “blockchain”?
2. Does a freelancer feel protected too, not accused?
3. Do users understand every fee, deadline and release rule before depositing cUSD?
4. Can they distinguish AI preparation, human review and contract settlement?
5. Does “Pay with proof” feel true at every point in the product experience?

## Final recommendation

Reclaim must lead with **protected commerce**, not recovery, dispute, DeFi, escrow or agent novelty.

> **The payment is the product. Protection is built into it.**
